Types of Wedding Catering Setups

Understanding different catering styles helps you choose what works best for your wedding vision, guest count, and budget:

Traditional Buffet Service

Best For: Large weddings (300+ guests), cost-effective service, variety of dishes

The most common setup at Indian weddings. Guests serve themselves from multiple food stations featuring appetisers, main courses, breads, rice, desserts, and beverages.

Pro Tip: Ensure sufficient buffet counters to avoid long queues — one counter per 75-100 guests is ideal.

Live Food Stations & Counters

Best For: Interactive experience, premium weddings, food enthusiasts

Chefs prepare dishes live in front of guests — chaat counters, dosa stations, pasta bars, tandoor grills, ice cream rolls, and more. Creates theatre and ensures maximum freshness.

Popular in Ranchi: Live chaat counter, tandoori kebab station, South Indian dosa corner, Chinese wok station

Plated Service (Sit-Down Dinner)

Best For: Intimate weddings (50-150 guests), formal receptions, luxury events

Guests are served pre-plated courses at their tables — typically a multi-course meal with appetiser, soup, main course, and dessert. Requires more staff but creates an elegant, fine-dining experience.

Fusion & Themed Menus

Best For: Modern couples, destination weddings, unique culinary experiences

Blending Indian and international cuisines — Indo-Chinese, Indian-Italian fusion, global street food stations, regional Indian thalis from different states, and more.

Must-Have Dishes for a Jharkhand Wedding Menu

A wedding in Ranchi should honour Jharkhand's rich culinary heritage while offering variety for guests from across India. Here's how to balance local flavours with broader appeal:

Traditional Jharkhand Dishes to Include:

  • • Dhuska: A beloved Jharkhand snack made from rice and lentil batter, deep-fried and served with aloo sabzi or chutney
  • • Litti Chokha: Roasted wheat balls stuffed with sattu, served with mashed vegetables — a regional favourite
  • • Rugra (Mushroom Curry): Wild mushroom curry unique to Jharkhand's tribal cuisine
  • • Bamboo Shoot Curry: Traditional tribal preparation with authentic local flavours
  • • Thekua: Traditional sweet made from wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee — often served at Chhath Puja and weddings

Pan-Indian Favourites for Broader Appeal:

North Indian: Paneer tikka, dal makhani, butter chicken, naan, biryani, raita

South Indian: Dosa, idli, sambhar, coconut chutney, filter coffee

Chaat & Street Food: Pani puri, bhel puri, aloo tikki, papdi chaat

Chinese-Indian: Manchurian, fried rice, hakka noodles, spring rolls

Desserts: Gulab jamun, rasgulla, jalebi, kulfi, ice cream counters

Pro Tip from White Horse Event Management

Balance is key. Include 2-3 signature Jharkhand dishes to honour local culture, but ensure 70-80% of your menu features widely loved pan-Indian options. This ensures every guest finds something they love.

Top Questions to Ask Any Caterer Before Booking

Before you sign a contract with any wedding caterer in Ranchi, ask these essential questions:

1. How many weddings of our size have you catered in the past year?

Experience at your specific scale matters. A caterer who regularly handles 500-guest weddings will be far more reliable than one who typically does 100-guest events.

2. Can we schedule a tasting session before finalising the menu?

Never book a caterer without tasting their food first. Professional caterers offer tastings as standard practice.

3. What is included in your per-plate pricing?

Clarify exactly what's covered — number of dishes, service staff, crockery, serving equipment, setup/cleanup, taxes, and any additional charges.

4. How do you handle dietary restrictions and allergies?

Ask about vegetarian, vegan, Jain, gluten-free, and allergy-safe options. Professional caterers should handle these seamlessly.

5. What is your staff-to-guest ratio?

Adequate service staff ensures smooth operations. Industry standard is 1 server per 25-30 guests for buffet service, 1 per 10-12 for plated service.

6. Do you have backup plans for equipment failure or vendor no-shows?

Professional caterers have contingency protocols, backup equipment, and emergency vendor contacts.

7. What are your food safety and hygiene certifications?

Ask to see FSSAI licenses, health department certifications, and hygiene protocols. This is non-negotiable.

8. What is your cancellation and refund policy?

Understand the terms clearly — advance payment requirements, cancellation deadlines, and refund conditions.

9. Can you provide references from recent weddings?

Speaking with past clients gives you real insights into reliability, quality, and professionalism.

10. How do you ensure food stays hot and fresh throughout service?

Ask about chafing dishes, warming equipment, staggered cooking schedules, and quality control during service.

2026 Catering Trends at Indian Weddings

The wedding catering landscape is evolving. Here are the trends shaping 2026:

Live ice cream rolls, Belgian waffle stations, French macaron towers, artisan chocolate fountains, and fusion desserts are complementing traditional Indian mithai.

Red Flags: Warning Signs of an Unreliable Caterer

Protect yourself by watching for these warning signs:

  • ❌ Refuses to provide a tasting: No professional caterer should ask you to book without tasting their food first
  • ❌ Vague or unclear pricing: Hidden charges, unclear inclusions, or reluctance to provide itemised quotes
  • ❌ No verifiable references: Cannot provide contact details of recent clients or has consistently negative reviews
  • ❌ Lack of proper licenses: No FSSAI certification or health department approvals
  • ❌ Poor communication: Slow to respond, vague answers, or unprofessional behaviour
  • ❌ Unrealistic promises: Prices far below market rates or guarantees that sound too good to be true
  • ❌ No written contract: Insists on verbal agreements or provides contracts with unclear terms
